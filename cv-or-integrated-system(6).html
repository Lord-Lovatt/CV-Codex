4. Pre-Bypass 0/14
‚ñº
PRE-BYPASS

Induction

  • Pre-oxygenation complete (3+ min)
  • Induction drugs given per protocol
  • Intubation confirmed (ETCO2, bilateral breath sounds)
  • ETT secured at appropriate depth

Central Line

  • CVL placed per surgeon preference
  • Swan-Ganz placed if indicated (attending has to be present in OR)
  • All lumens aspirate and flush

Pre-Heparin

  • TXA loading dose given (1g over 20 min)
  • Baseline Labs & ACT obtained
  • TEE probe placed (if indicated)
  • Prepare Heparin dose (300-400 U/kg)
  • Communicate with perfusionist

ERAS Anesthesia Protocol

  • Ketamine 25‚Äì50 mg IV PRE-BYPASS
  • TXA gtt Running at 2 mg/kg/hr
HEPARIN: Given on surgeon command. Confirm dose verbally. Target ACT >450
5. On Bypass 0/8
‚ñº
ON BYPASS

Initiation

  • Confirm ACT >450 before cannulation
  • Turn off vent/alarms and announce SEVO OFF for perfusion to dial theirs
  • Observe for proper venous drainage

Monitoring During Bypass

  • Monitor MAP (per surgeon preference, typically 60-80)
  • ACT/Labs will be checked by perfusion, ensure you have the data
  • TXA maintenance infusion running
  • Document all events and interventions

Preparation for Separation

  • Prepare inotropes/vasopressors per surgeon preference
TIP: Check Surgeon Prefs tab for specific drip preferences for your surgeon
6. Off Bypass 0/23
‚ñº
POST-BYPASS

Pre-Separation

  • All required drips running (pressors preferred after aortic clamp off)
  • Call Attending MD for TEE evaluation
  • Confirm all data sources flowing (FloTrac, CVP) and SEVO back ON

Separation Protocol

  • MANDATORY: Cleared by Surgeon AND Perfusionist
  • Initiate Protamine (test dose recommended)
  • Maintain clear spoken 3-way communication
  • ANCEF given (every 4 hours, or after bypass), whichever comes first

Post-Bypass Labs

  • Perform ALL labs and ACT seven (7) minutes after Protamine infused
  • Post-wires labs strongly encouraged
Goal: Do not bring acidotic patient to CVICU. If correcting multiple lab values concurrently ‚Üí check more frequently.

Final Steps in OR

  • TEE removed (call Attending for permission)
  • Place OG tube
  • Change CVL dressing (sterile technique) if needed

ERAS Post-Bypass Anesthesia

  • Ketamine 25‚Äì50 mg IV POST-BYPASS

ERAS Surgical (Exparel)

  • Exparel administered at chest tube sites
  • Exparel administered at drain sites

ERAS Regional Blocks (End of Case)

BLOCKS: PEC 1 / PEC 2 and Serratus blocks
MEDICATION: Bupivacaine 0.25% + epi 1:200K (40 ml)
  • Enter patient name into ultrasound machine
  • Prime tubing for block
  • Assist with medication injection
  • PEC 1 block completed
  • PEC 2 block completed
  • Serratus block completed
  • Block images captured and documented
